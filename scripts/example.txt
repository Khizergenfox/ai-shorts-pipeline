Most people building with AI assume the model is the bottleneck.

It isn't.

The bottleneck is what you ask the model to do. The same model with the right framing produces work that looks ten times better than the same model with a vague prompt.

So if your AI output feels generic, before you switch models, switch your prompt.

Two changes that worked for us. First, give the model the constraint, not the goal — "produce a 60-second video script" is a goal; "produce a 60-second script in thinking-flow prose, no list-style sentences, with one contrarian angle in the second beat" is a constraint. Constraints produce surprises. Goals produce averages.

Second, show the model an example of the output you actually want. Not what you mean by "good" — a real piece of writing or code that you'd ship. The model anchors on examples far more than on adjectives.

That's it. Same model, sharper output. Try it on tomorrow's task.
